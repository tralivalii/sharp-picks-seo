Value Betting vs. Chasing Losses: Why 90% of Bettors Fail

The Chasing Trap

After a loss, most bettors instinctively want to bet bigger to "win it back." This is the single fastest way to go broke. Chasing losses leads to poor decisions, oversized bets, and emotional gambling.

What Is Value Betting?

A value bet exists when you believe the true probability of an outcome is higher than what the odds suggest. Example: a team at 2.00 odds has implied probability of 50%. If you calculate their true win chance at 60%, that's a value bet.

How to Find Value

  • Compare odds across multiple bookmakers
  • Use statistical models (xG, possession, form)
  • Focus on markets where you have an edge
  • Track your predictions and adjust

Why Most Bettors Lose

The most comprehensive study of betting accounts showed 97% of bettors lose over a 12-month period. The primary reasons: poor bankroll management, emotional betting, and chasing losses.
