How to Spot a Scam Capper: Red Flags and Warning Signs

The Scale of the Problem

Our research analyzed 50+ Telegram "capper" channels. The findings: 80% show evidence of fabricated statistics, deleted losing posts, or unrealistic win rates.

Red Flag #1: Claiming 90%+ Win Rates

No legitimate analyst maintains 90% accuracy over meaningful sample sizes. Even the best tipsters achieve 60-70% when using low odds, and 50-55% at even money.

Red Flag #2: Deleting Losing Posts

If predictions are only visible when they win, the "analyst" is curating their results. Real transparency means keeping every pick visible.

Red Flag #3: Pressuring for Affiliate Sign-Ups

Many capper channels exist solely to drive traffic to specific bookmakers via affiliate links. Their "analysis" is secondary to their affiliate revenue.
