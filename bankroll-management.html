Bankroll Management 101: How Professional Bettors Protect Their Capital

Why Bankroll Management Matters More Than Picks

A bettor with 60% accuracy who bets 10% per play will go broke faster than a bettor with 50% accuracy who bets 1% per play. It's not about how often you win — it's about how you size your bets.

The Kelly Criterion Simplified

The Kelly Criterion is the mathematical formula for optimal stake sizing. But it's too aggressive for most bettors. We recommend flat betting at 2-5% per play.

Simple Rules

  • Set aside a dedicated betting bankroll
  • Never bet more than 5% on a single play
  • After a losing streak, reduce stakes by 50%
  • After a winning streak, maintain same stakes
  • Track every single bet in a spreadsheet

The Psychology of Drawdowns

A 10-bet losing streak at 60% accuracy happens about once every 200 bets. That's normal. What separates professionals from amateurs is how they react — pros stay calm and follow their system.

Recommended Bankroll Sizes

  • Casual bettor: $200-500 (unit size $5-10)
  • Serious bettor: $1,000-5,000 (unit size $20-100)
  • Professional: $10,000+ (unit size $200-500)
