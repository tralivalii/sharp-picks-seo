Betting Odds Explained: What They Actually Mean

What Are Betting Odds?

Betting odds represent two things: the payout you'll receive if your bet wins, and the implied probability of that outcome occurring.

Decimal Odds (European)

Most common format. Example: odds of 1.72 mean a $100 bet returns $172 ($100 stake + $72 profit). The implied probability is 1/1.72 = 58.1%.

Implied Probability

To calculate implied probability: 1 / decimal odds × 100. If odds are 2.00, the market says there's a 50% chance. If your analysis says 60%, you've found value.

The Bookmaker's Edge (Vig)

Bookmakers build a margin into every market. On a fair coin flip (50/50), odds would be 2.00 each. In reality, you'll see 1.91 each — that 4.5% difference is the bookmaker's profit margin.

How We Use Odds

We compare odds across multiple bookmakers and calculate true probability using Pinnacle's sharp lines as reference. When we find odds significantly above fair value, that's our signal.
